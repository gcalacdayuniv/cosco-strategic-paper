# Chapter 5: Strategies in Action

## Long-Term Objectives (2026–2029)
* Turnaround from a P5.58 million net loss to a P3 million net income by FY 2027.
* Scale annual sales revenue from P28.7 million to P45 million by FY 2029.
* Reduce trade payables (P32.1 million) by 25% over three years.
* Transition to full production automation (CNC/robotics) by FY 2028.

## Integration Strategies
* **Backward Integration:** Securing exclusive contracts or acquiring local wood/metal suppliers to stabilize procurement costs and protect against global inflation.
* **Forward Integration:** Utilizing direct digital marketing to bypass intermediaries and capture full profit margins on major government institutional contracts.
* **Horizontal Integration:** Consolidating regional design shops in Cavite/Manila to achieve economies of scale required for factory automation.

## Intensive Strategies
* **Market Penetration:** Aggressively bidding on the P72.05 million SSS 2025 Annual Procurement Plan using existing P17.3 million raw material inventory.
* **Market Development:** Expanding geographic reach beyond Cavite/Metro Manila into high-growth provincial hubs like Cebu and Davao, while targeting compact home-office segments.
* **Product Development:** Commercializing the "Innovating the Future" vision by integrating smart technology (wireless charging, AV setups), ergonomic height adjustments, and sustainable biophilic materials (bamboo, reclaimed wood).

## Diversification Strategies
* **Related Diversification:** Manufacturing eco-friendly acoustic panels and offering workplace wellness assessment consulting services.
* **Unrelated Diversification:** Providing commercial facility management for institutional clients or repurposing logistics assets for industrial warehousing.

## Defensive Strategies
* **Retrenchment:** Trimming P7.7 million in general administrative overhead and leaning out manufacturing processes to reverse profit declines.
* **Divestiture / Liquidation:** Selling non-performing trading divisions or surplus equipment as a last resort to settle trade payables if solvency worsens.

## Porter's Five Generic Strategies
Cosco must pivot away from broad Cost Leadership (Type 1) and adopt **Type 5: Best-Value Focus (Focused Differentiation)**. By combining bespoke design differentiation with targeted production automation, Cosco serves high-value institutional niches (e.g., SSS procurement) with superior price-value structures.

## Means for Achieving Strategies
* **Cooperation / Joint Ventures:** Partnering with tech firms for smart furniture integration and collaborating with local rivals to lobby against trade tariffs.
* **First-Mover Advantage:** Leading the Philippine furniture sector in early adoption of CNC/robotic assembly and 100% circular-economy green practices.
* **Outsourcing:** Subcontracting outbound logistics delivery fleets and MIS data operations to specialized BPO providers to reduce fixed administrative overhead.
