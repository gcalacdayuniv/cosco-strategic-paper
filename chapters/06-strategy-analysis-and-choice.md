# Chapter 6: Strategy Analysis and Choice

## TOWS Strategic Mapping
* **SO Strategies:** Leverage bespoke design and 24% sales growth to capture the P72.05M SSS procurement contract and launch biophilic wellness lines.
* **WO Strategies:** Invest in production automation (CNC/robotics) to reduce the 92% cost-of-sales ratio; use digital platforms to liquidate the P17.3M inventory bottleneck.
* **ST Strategies:** Utilize executive leadership to negotiate inflationary supplier terms and leverage tax compliance records to win corporate trust over 250+ rivals.
* **WT Strategies:** Execute defensive retrenchment to reduce administrative overhead and adopt lean manufacturing to survive price wars.

## SPACE Matrix Analysis
* **Coordinates:** X-Axis (+1.25), Y-Axis (-1.50) $\rightarrow$ **Resultant Vector: (+1.25, -1.50)**
* **Posture: Competitive Quadrant (Bottom-Right).** Cosco operates in an attractive, high-growth domestic market but suffers from internal financial weakness (P29.5M deficit). Strategic imperative: improve production efficiency and cash flow.

## Portfolio Analysis (BCG & IE Matrices)
* **BCG Matrix:** Both Bespoke Manufacturing and Trading divisions map as **Question Marks**. They sit in high-growth markets but hold low relative market share and consume heavy cash.
* **IE Matrix:** Placed firmly in **Cell V (Hold and Maintain)** based on IFE (2.55) and EFE (2.65) scores. Recommended focus: intensive market penetration and product development.
* **Grand Strategy Matrix:** Bespoke Manufacturing sits in **Quadrant I** (Rapid Growth / Strong Competitive Position), justifying aggressive growth investments. The Trading Division sits in **Quadrant II** (Rapid Growth / Weak Position), requiring digital partnerships or divestiture.

## Quantitative Strategic Planning Matrix (QSPM)
Evaluating three core strategic alternatives across Cosco's complete SWOT profile:
1. **Product Development (Total Attractiveness Score: 5.60) — PRIORITY 1:** Developing biophilic, smart, and ergonomic furniture commands premium prices that protect thin margins from inflation and avoid broad price wars.
2. **Market Penetration (Total Attractiveness Score: 4.90) — PRIORITY 2:** Bidding on large institutional contracts (SSS) leverages core know-how and rapidly converts the P17.3M raw material inventory into liquid cash flow.
3. **Related Diversification (Total Attractiveness Score: 3.31) — LEAST ATTRACTIVE:** Entering new service sectors risks overextending stockholder advances during severe capital deficiency.
