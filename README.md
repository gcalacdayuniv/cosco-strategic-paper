# Strategic Management Paper: Cosco Integrated Office Systems, Inc.

This repository contains the comprehensive Strategic Management Paper for **Cosco Integrated Office Systems, Inc.**, developed as partial fulfillment of the requirements for the Master's in Business Administration (MBA 648: Strategic Management) at Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila (University of the City of Manila).

## Repository Structure & Chapter Index

The project follows a modular file structure where each chapter is maintained independently within the `/chapters` directory to facilitate clear analysis and updates.

### [Chapter 1: Executive Summary](./chapters/01-executive-summary.md)
Provides a high-level overview of Cosco Integrated Office Systems, Inc., summarizing the operational challenges, financial distress (including the P5.58 million net loss and P29.5 million cumulative deficit in 2025), and key strategic recommendations to achieve turnaround and profitability.

### [Chapter 2: Mission, Vision, and Values](./chapters/02-mission-vision-values.md)
Examines the corporate identity of Cosco, evaluating its core philosophies and strategic direction anchored on its core vision: *"Innovating the Future."*

### [Chapter 3: External Environment Analysis](./chapters/03-external-environment-analysis.md)
Analyzes macro-environmental and micro-environmental factors impacting the Philippine furniture sector, including:
* PESTLE Analysis (Economic inflation, U.S. tariff risks, sustainability trends)
* Porter's Five Forces Analysis (Intense rivalry among 250+ local and global brands)
* Industry Growth Roadmap (Targeting 8% annual growth leading to 2030)

### [Chapter 4: Internal Environment Analysis](./chapters/04-internal-environment-analysis.md)
Audits Cosco's functional areas (Management, Marketing, Finance, Production, and MIS) to evaluate core competencies and operational bottlenecks:
* Leadership oversight under COO Linda V. Dela Cruz
* Assessment of the 92% cost-of-sales ratio and P32.1 million in trade payables
* Analysis of the P17.3 million raw material inventory bottleneck

### [Chapter 5: Strategies in Action](./chapters/05-strategies-in-action.md)
Identifies actionable, long-term strategic initiatives designed to stabilize operations and capture high-value markets:
* **Long-Term Objectives:** Reversing net losses to achieve P3 million net income by 2027 and scaling revenue to P45 million by 2029.
* **Integration Strategies:** Forward (Direct digital marketing), Backward (Locking in raw material suppliers), and Horizontal integration.
* **Intensive Strategies:** Market Penetration (Targeting massive government bids like the P72.05M SSS procurement plan), Market Development (Provincial expansion to Cebu and Davao), and Product Development (Biophilic, smart, and ergonomic wellness furniture).
* **Diversification Strategies:** Related (Sustainable acoustic panels, wellness consulting) and Unrelated diversification (Facility management, industrial logistics).
* **Defensive Strategies:** Retrenchment, Divestiture, and Liquidation thresholds.
* **Porter's Five Generic Strategies:** Strategic shift toward **Type 5: Best-Value Focus (Focused Differentiation)**.
* **Achieving Strategies:** Cooperation, Joint Ventures, First-Mover advantages in production automation (CNC and robotics), and Outsourcing non-core MIS/logistics functions.

### [Chapter 6: Strategy Analysis and Choice](./chapters/06-strategy-analysis-and-choice.md)
Applies a rigorous analytical framework to match internal capabilities with external market opportunities:
* **Input Stage:**
  * External Factor Evaluation (EFE) Matrix - Score: **2.65**
  * Competitive Profile Matrix (CPM) - Score: **2.64** (Compared against Volume Wholesalers and Global Design Leaders)
  * Key Success Factors (KSFs)
  * Internal Factor Evaluation (IFE) Matrix - Score: **2.55**
* **Matching Stage:**
  * TOWS Matrix (SO, WO, ST, and WT strategic pairings)
  * Strategic Position and Action Evaluation (SPACE) Matrix - Resultant Vector: **(+1.25, -1.50)** landing in the **Competitive Quadrant**
  * Boston Consulting Group (BCG) Matrix - Evaluating Bespoke Manufacturing and Trading divisions (Both plotted as **Question Marks**)
  * Internal-External (IE) Matrix - Plotted in **Cell V (Hold and Maintain)**
  * Grand Strategy Matrix - Bespoke Manufacturing in **Quadrant I**; Trading Division in **Quadrant II**
* **Decision Stage:**
  * Quantitative Strategic Planning Matrix (QSPM) - Evaluating Market Penetration (TAS: 4.90), Product Development (**TAS: 5.60**), and Related Diversification (TAS: 3.31). Final recommendation prioritizes **Product Development**.

### [Chapter 7: Strategy Implementation](./chapters/07-strategy-implementation.md)
Outlines the operationalization of selected strategies, detailing resource allocation, organizational restructuring, production automation timelines (2026 to 2032), and change management protocols required to execute the turnaround.

### [Chapter 8: Strategy Evaluation and Control](./chapters/08-strategy-evaluation-and-control.md)
Establishes the performance monitoring frameworks, continuous auditing mechanisms, and corrective feedback loops needed to measure strategic success against financial milestones and inventory turnover targets.
